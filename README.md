# Netflix Global Content Strategy Analysis

## Objective
To analyze Netflix's catalog evolution over the last decade using a dataset of 8,807 titles[cite: 6]. The goal is to understand how the platform pivoted from a content aggregator to a global production powerhouse by examining regional distribution, audience targeting, and retention mechanics[cite: 6].

## Tech Stack
*   **Python:** Data cleaning and Exploratory Data Analysis using Pandas, Seaborn, and Plotly[cite: 6, 7].
*   **Visualization:** Interactive Power BI dashboard highlighting catalog scale-up and regional distribution[cite: 5].

## Key Insights
*   **The "Originals" Pivot:** Post-2016 data reveals a dramatic reduction in the lag time between a title's original release and its addition to Netflix, confirming an aggressive shift toward Netflix Originals to compete with cinema releases[cite: 6].
*   **Global Expansion:** The United States remains the primary content engine with over 3,600 titles[cite: 6]. India has emerged as the second-largest contributor, heavily skewed toward acquiring high-volume Bollywood movies (90% Movies vs. 10% TV Shows)[cite: 6].
*   **Audience Targeting:** The platform is heavily skewed toward mature audiences, with over 65% of the catalog rated TV-MA or R[cite: 6]. Content rated for children represents a minor fraction of the library[cite: 6].
*   **Retention Mechanics:** Over 60% of TV Shows end after Season 1, indicating a high-risk strategy of cancelling underperformers to fund new hits[cite: 6]. Content additions spike consistently in July and December to minimize churn during high-viewership windows[cite: 6].

## Strategic Recommendations
*   **Mitigate Cancellation Fatigue:** Explicitly market one-season shows as "Limited Series" to manage audience expectations and prevent user hesitation when committing to new series[cite: 6].
*   **Evolve Regional Strategy:** Shift the Indian market budget toward multi-season TV series to build long-term subscriber loyalty, replicating the retention success of K-Dramas[cite: 6].
*   **Defend the Family Segment:** Increase investment in animated franchises and family-friendly content to prevent households with children from churning to competitors[cite: 6].
